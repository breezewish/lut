## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** The product does not read as AI-generated. Its restrained dark editing register follows the task instead of a decorative template: no card grid, glass, gradient text, oversized metric, repeated eyebrow, gratuitous motion, or invented control.

**Deterministic scan:** Both the layout-scoped and full detectors returned zero findings for `web/src`. The documented arbitrary-spacing and arbitrary-z-index grep also returned no matches. The scan caught no issue omitted by the visual review and produced no false positive.

**Visual overlays:** No user-visible overlay was injected. Headless Chromium inspection covered rendered desktop empty/ready, mobile empty/ready, and short-viewport states; the clean CLI scan left no finding to overlay.

## Overall Impression

The interface feels like a focused color tool: file context at the edge, editing controls above the image, comparison as the dominant surface, and output after the decision. The strongest opportunity was behavioral rather than visual: export readiness now follows the exact processed preview instead of merely the presence of a selected file.

## What's Working

- `web/src/App.tsx` orders controls, comparison, and output exactly as the user journey progresses, while empty state removes irrelevant editing and export decisions.
- `web/src/styles.css` uses the documented 4/8/12/16/24/32/48 spacing tokens with tight control groupings and generous image space; `.comparison` receives the flexible dominant area.
- `.preview-grid` is the only coordinated two-dimensional content grid. Queue rows and controls use one-dimensional flex layouts, and responsive rules at 52rem and 35rem structurally reshape the workspace.

## Layout Verification

- **Spacing:** `--space-2xs` through `--space-2xl` constrain spacing values; 8–12px groups labels and controls while 16–24px separates panels and comparison content.
- **Visual hierarchy:** `.comparison { flex: 1 }` and the two large preview canvases dominate after the compact `.control-bar`; `.output-bar` closes the task flow.
- **Grid and structure:** `.app-grid` coordinates the fixed queue and flexible workspace, while `.preview-grid` coordinates only the two comparable images. No repeated or nested card grid exists.
- **Rhythm and variety:** the queue is dense, controls are compact, previews are expansive, and the output group returns to compact density. Surface changes reinforce these transitions without borders or shadows.
- **Density:** 44px controls and compact 12–14px metadata suit a production tool, while canvas minimums and flexible growth protect image inspection.
- **Squint test:** the rose primary action and two bright images remain first and second in visual weight; selected queue context is the clear tertiary group.
- **Breathing room:** `.comparison` uses 24px outer spacing and 12px between panes on desktop; mobile reduces side padding to 16px without crowding controls.
- **Consistency:** the same tokens, button vocabulary, radii, and state colors apply across topbar, queue, controls, banners, and output.
- **Responsiveness:** rendered 1440×960, 390×844, and 1024×600 states confirm the sidebar-to-row transition, stacked mobile previews, full-width mobile controls, and reachable output through page scrolling.
