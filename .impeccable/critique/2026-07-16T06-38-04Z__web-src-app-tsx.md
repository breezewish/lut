# RAW Alchemy Web Critique

## Executive Summary

RAW Alchemy presents a focused, professional darkroom workspace whose primary hierarchy is the image comparison rather than decorative UI. The review found two concrete polish issues: the medium-width control bar consumed too much vertical space, and the LUT search field described itself as search even though it filters the adjacent selector. Both were resolved during this pass. No unresolved P0-P3 issue remains.

The interface scores **38/40** across Nielsen's ten heuristics. The two points withheld are for power-user efficiency and contextual help: the product intentionally has no keyboard workflow or dedicated help surface yet, while its inline guidance is otherwise strong.

## What Works

- The image pair is visually dominant, with restrained chrome and a flat surface hierarchy.
- Spacing follows the product's 4/8/12/16/24/32/48 rhythm, using tighter gaps inside controls and wider separation between semantic groups.
- Queue, processing, export, completion, and failure states remain visible in context.
- Destructive and interruptive actions are explicit: remove, clear, undo, reset, and stop-after-current.
- Photography concepts are named in domain language, while the undeclared output profile is disclosed instead of hidden.
- Mobile preserves task order: queue, controls, comparison, export, then technical note.
- Focus, disabled, hover, success, and error treatments are consistent and accessible.

## Priority Issues

No unresolved P0-P3 issue remains.

Resolved during the critique:

1. **P2 — Medium-width control density.** At 768 x 900 and 1024 x 600, semantic control groups wrapped into a 239px-tall stack. Narrower flex bases and exposure width keep them on one 128px row when space is sufficient, while the existing 390px mobile stack remains unchanged.
2. **P3 — LUT field semantics.** The field filters the adjacent Look selector. Its placeholder now says “Filter 27 looks,” making the relationship explicit without adding another interaction model.

## Detailed Findings

### Spatial composition

The desktop split between queue and workspace is stable. The responsive transition at 52rem moves the queue above the workspace, which gives the comparison the full width instead of compressing two image panes. At 768 x 900, the corrected control row ends at y=356, the comparison ends at y=728, and the export bar ends at y=796. The 900px document has no horizontal overflow and does not require vertical scrolling.

At 1024 x 600, the corrected control row ends at y=200 and the comparison at y=540. The export bar begins within the viewport and is fully reachable with a minimal scroll. At 390 x 844, the intentional single-column control and comparison stacks remain unchanged; the 390px document has no horizontal overflow.

### Hierarchy and rhythm

The two preview images carry the strongest visual weight. Labels, metadata, assumptions, and technical notes use quieter color and compact type. The queue selection color and primary export color are reused consistently. Sections do not become repetitive cards: grouping comes from alignment, surface changes, and spacing.

### Interaction clarity

The chooser and Add RAWs affordance are visible in the empty state. The ready state keeps Look, Exposure, Reset, and Export selected together. Queue status text and progress remain adjacent to the affected file. The filtered selector keeps the selected Look visible and keyboard accessible.
